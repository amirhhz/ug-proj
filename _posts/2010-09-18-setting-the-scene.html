So, it's September and a few weeks until the Cambridge academic term begins. I have been wisely informed that a good student ought to have his dissertation proposal written by start of term. I'm having a go at being an even better student by making a start on the actual project before then, and here I'll just give the preamble to the current state of affairs.

In the summer of 2009, I interned at Mixcloud with CTO Mat Clayton for a while. I saw how exciting it was being part of a fresh enterprise (Mixcloud publicly launched just a few weeks after I left) and learnt a bunch of stuff that's not relevant for now, but I might write about later. At the end of my time there, a few ideas for my final year project came up and the one that struck a chord with me was writing a recommendation engine (or as some prefer, recommender system) for Mixcloud. I'd always viewed recommendations on sites like Amazon and Facebook with mixed feelings - part fascinated ("Wow, yes, I DO want to buy a tie-rack, too! Thanks, Mr. Bezos, you're a mind reader!"), part violated ("No, I don't Like the overhyped non-celebrities that my so-called friends have given consistent thumbs-ups to")Â  - but I certainly was curious about them.

Think of Mixcloud as "YouTube of Radio" - their own tagline. Now think of Twitter on top of that. You have users uploading audio content aka 'Cloudcasts', listening, following each other, favouriting, commenting, etc. with the result being a great, young and growing online community of audio producers and listeners. To make this an analyst's dream, the site encourages (forces, even) uploaders to provide a rich set of metadata for each piece of content.

So a year later, in which the website has had incredible growth, I am raring to get going with my project. I sent around a draft of my proposal to people at the Computer Labs who I thought may be interested in supervising my project, or at least not mind doing so, and I was surprised and pleased to land myself the perfect supervisor for this field in a great team. After a meeting or two, I have some ideas as to how to move forward, and the next action is data-gathering. Luckily, Mixcloud quietly released their API not long ago which, with their consent, shall be my source of data. There are alternative datasets (e.g. movies), but Mixcloud is what makes this project exciting for me.

At this stage, I'm looking forward to doing the following, and I'll write about each as and when appropriate:

  • Writing my first data scraper, if my data is to come from the Web or an API
  • Shaping the data into some sensible format for storage and analysis, most likely straight from JSON to CouchDB or MongoDB
  • Producing a list of techniques and algorithms that I could feasibly use to produce the recommendations
  • Given all the above, draft an evaluation strategy
  • Ensuring any legal agreements necessary are signed through
  • Finalizing and clarifying my proposal (then I can link to it from this blog!)

Six things. That should be fine.
